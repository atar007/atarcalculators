Quick answers

How is HSC Maths Extension 2 scaled?

UAC scales your raw Maths Extension 2 mark against how the whole Maths Extension 2 cohort performs across all of their subjects. A strong cohort lifts a subject; a broader one lowers it. Maths Extension 2's average scaled mark is about 43.4/50 per unit versus roughly 25 across all subjects. So it scales up.

Does Maths Extension 2 scale up or down?

Relative to other subjects, Maths Extension 2 scales up. Its cohort's average scaled mark is about 43.4/50 per unit, against about 25 across the whole state. HSC marks are reported high. Most students sit in Bands 4 to 6. So scaled marks come out lower than HSC marks in almost every subject. What matters is how a subject's scaled marks compare with others. The raw drop in the number is not the point.

What's a good HSC Maths Extension 2 mark?

The median student scores about 43/50, which scales to roughly 44.7/50. Beat that and you're in the top half of the cohort; a mark in the top 10% sits near 48/50.

What raw mark do I need for an E4 in Maths Extension 2?

An E4 is the top band (45–50) in this 1-unit course. NESA sets the raw cut-off each year by aligning to standards, so it shifts annually. This tool estimates your scaled mark, which is what feeds your ATAR.

How Maths Extension 2 scaling works

Scaling is not a penalty or a bonus. It puts every HSC subject on the same footing. So a mark in Maths Extension 2 can be compared fairly with a mark in any other subject. Here is exactly how your raw Maths Extension 2 mark becomes a scaled mark.

The formula

Mark per unit = your raw Maths Extension 2 mark ÷ 2 (it is a 2-unit course) Scaled mark (per unit) = mark per unit → UAC scaling curve Your ATAR uses the scaled mark, counted across your best 10 units A raw mark of 100 does not become a scaled mark of 50. Scaling reflects how the Maths Extension 2 cohort performed across all their subjects, so the scaled mark can sit above or below the raw mark per unit.

Step by step

  1. Take your raw mark out of 100. Enter your actual or predicted Maths Extension 2 HSC mark. Predicted marks are fine before results day.
  2. Convert to a per-unit mark. Maths Extension 2 is a 2-unit course. So your mark out of 100 is treated as a mark per unit (out of 50) for scaling.
  3. Apply the scaling curve. UAC maps your per-unit mark onto the Maths Extension 2 scaling curve for the year, drawn from the official percentile distribution. This is done automatically here.
  4. Read your scaled mark. You get a scaled mark per unit (out of 50). Multiply by 2 for the subject’s full contribution to your best-10-units ATAR aggregate.

Common mistakes with Maths Extension 2 scaling

These are the misunderstandings that trip students up most often.

  • Thinking a raw 100 becomes a scaled 50. Scaling is a rank-based adjustment, not a straight percentage. Even a very high Maths Extension 2 mark scales to a value set by the whole cohort, not automatically to the top.
  • Assuming Maths Extension 2 scaling is fixed forever. The curve is recalculated each year from that year’s cohort. Last year’s numbers are a close guide, not a guarantee.
  • Confusing your scaled mark with your ATAR. The scaled mark is one subject’s contribution. Your ATAR comes from your best 10 units across all subjects, then a rank.
  • Picking Maths Extension 2 only because it "scales up". A higher-scaling subject only helps if you can score well in it. A strong mark in a slightly lower-scaling subject usually beats a weak mark in a higher-scaling one.
  • Forgetting the 2-unit doubling. Your scaled mark per unit counts twice in the aggregate. When comparing subjects, compare the full 2-unit contribution, not just the per-unit figure.

HSC Maths Extension 2 scaling — common questions

I got 45 in HSC Maths Extension 2 — what scaled mark is that?

A raw HSC mark of 45 in Maths Extension 2 scales to roughly 46 out of 50 (a per-unit scaled mark). For comparison, a raw 40 scales to about 43, and a raw 48 to about 48. These are estimates from the latest UAC scaling pattern — the exact figure shifts a little each year with the cohort.

Does HSC Maths Extension 2 scale up or down?

Maths Extension 2 is one of the stronger-scaling HSC subjects. Its median student's scaled mark is about 45/50, well above the roughly 25/50 state average, because the Maths Extension 2 cohort is academically strong. In absolute terms, a scaled mark still sits below the raw HSC mark through the middle of the range. That happens in almost every subject. But Maths Extension 2 loses far less than weaker-scaling subjects. That is what people mean when they say it ‘scales up’.

Why is my scaled mark lower than my HSC mark in Maths Extension 2?

Because the two numbers measure different things. Your raw HSC mark (out of 50) is aligned to performance bands. Your scaled mark (out of 50 per unit) places you against every student in the state. It reflects how strong the Maths Extension 2 cohort is. A raw 45 in Maths Extension 2, for instance, becomes about 46/50. Scaled marks sitting below raw HSC marks in the middle of the range is normal and happens in nearly every subject.

What raw mark do I need in Maths Extension 2 for a scaled mark of 40?

A scaled mark of about 40/50 is a strong contribution to your ATAR aggregate. In Maths Extension 2, reaching it takes a raw HSC mark of roughly 36 out of 50. Lifting your raw mark above that pushes your scaled mark higher still, up to about 50/50 at the very top.

Is HSC Maths Extension 2 worth taking for a high ATAR?

From a pure scaling view, yes — Maths Extension 2 is among the better-scaling subjects. But the catch matters: scaling rewards the subject only if you actually perform well in it. A middling mark in Maths Extension 2 scales worse than a high mark in a 'weaker' subject. Choose it if it's a genuine strength, not just for the scaling.

What HSC mark puts me in the top 10% of Maths Extension 2?

Roughly the top 10% of Maths Extension 2 students score a raw HSC mark of about 48 or higher. Your position within the cohort is what drives your scaled mark — the stronger your rank, the higher your scaled result.

How is HSC Maths Extension 2 scaled for the ATAR?

UAC scales your Maths Extension 2 marks (exam plus moderated assessment) so they compare fairly with every other subject. The scaling reflects how academically strong the Maths Extension 2 cohort is — the stronger the field, the higher the subject scales. It's your scaled marks, not your raw HSC marks, that feed into your ATAR aggregate.
