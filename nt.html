How the NTCET ATAR calculation works

Your NTCET ATAR is not the average of your marks. It is a rank built in stages. Scale each result, add your best subjects into an aggregate, then convert that aggregate to a rank out of 99.95. Here is the exact method this calculator uses, following SATAC’s published model.

The formula

Scaled result = your subject result → scaled by SATAC Aggregate = your best 3 (in full) + half of a scaled 4th ATAR = your aggregate (out of 90) converted to a rank (0 to 99.95) The NTCET uses the SACE system, so SATAC counts your best three 20-credit subjects in full plus half of a scaled fourth, building an aggregate out of 90.

Step by step

  1. Get a result for each subject. The NTCET follows the SACE, so each Stage 2 subject gives a result that SATAC converts to scaled marks. A full 20-credit subject can contribute up to 20 scaled points.
  2. Scale each subject. SATAC scales your subjects so they compare fairly. Scaling is applied automatically.
  3. Take your best three, plus half a fourth. Your three best scaled 20-credit results count in full, with a further scaled result added at half weight.
  4. Form your aggregate. The counted results are summed to an aggregate out of a maximum of 90.
  5. Convert the aggregate to an ATAR. Your aggregate is ranked against the cohort to give your ATAR, from 0 to 99.95.

About this NT ATAR calculator

This tool estimates your NT ATAR. The NT uses the SACE system, called NTCET. SATAC scales and ranks it. Each Stage 2 subject gives you a scaled score out of 20. SATAC adds your best 90 credits of these scores. That total is your university aggregate, out of 90. We turn it into an ATAR. For the full method, see our methodology page.

NT and SA students are ranked together. They sit in one shared cohort. So the maths is the same as South Australia. Only Stage 2 results count towards your ATAR. You also need Research Project B. It is compulsory and worth 10 credits. Add it when you use this tool.

CDU is the main uni in the NT. It has campuses in Darwin and nearby towns. Many of its courses have lower cutoffs. It also runs a medical course with Flinders. The section above lists guide cutoffs.

SATAC sets your real ATAR. It arrives in mid to late December. This estimate gets you close. It cannot be exact yet. Scaling depends on this year's students. That data does not exist until results day. Use this to plan and aim.

NT students receive their ATAR through SATAC under SACE rules, so the official cut-offs and aggregation come from the South Australian system.

Common mistakes when estimating your NTCET ATAR

These are the errors that throw Territory estimates off most often. Two of them are specific to running on a borrowed framework.

  • Assuming the NTCET is its own system. It is not. The NTCET runs on the SACE framework, SATAC calculates it, and NT and SA students are ranked in one shared cohort. If you want the mechanics, South Australia’s rules are yours.
  • Treating the ATAR as an average of your marks. It is a rank. An ATAR of 90 means you rank in the top 10% — not that you averaged 90. In a small Territory cohort that distinction surprises people every year.
  • Forgetting Research Project B. It is compulsory and worth 10 credits. Students leave it out of the tool and wonder why their aggregate looks short.
  • Entering optimistic marks. Enter what you are genuinely tracking towards, then run a separate ambitious scenario. Two numbers are more useful than one hopeful one.
  • Adding bonus points to your ATAR. Adjustment factors do not change your ATAR. They change your selection rank for particular courses — and CDU runs its own schemes worth checking directly.

NT ATAR — common questions

How is the ATAR calculated in NT?

You get a result in each subject. SATAC scales each result against the state. Your best results are added into a university aggregate. That total is ranked against every student. Your place in that line becomes your ATAR. The ATAR runs from 0 to 99.95, but ATARs below 30.00 are reported as '30.00 or less'.

Is the NT ATAR the same as SA?

Yes. The NT uses the SACE system, called NTCET. SATAC works out both the same way. NT and SA students are ranked together in one cohort.

Why is my ATAR lower than my marks?

Your ATAR is a rank, not a mark. It shows where you placed against everyone else. Marks show what you scored. The two are not the same. Your marks are also scaled before ranking. So a high average can still give a lower ATAR. This is normal and happens to most students.

How accurate is this calculator before results day?

It uses the real method and the latest scaling data. Most estimates land within a few ATAR points. It cannot be exact yet. This year's scaling is not set until results come out. Use it as a guide, not a promise.

Which subjects scale up the most in NT?

Maths scales best. Specialist Maths and Methods are the top two. Physics, Chemistry and languages also scale well. English Literary Studies is the best English subject. A subject scales up when its students are strong across all subjects. But scaling only helps if you do well in that subject.

Does picking high-scaling subjects guarantee a high ATAR?

No. This is the biggest myth about the ATAR. Scaling only helps if you score well. A high mark in a subject you love beats a low mark in a 'high-scaling' one. Pick subjects you can do well in.

What's a good ATAR?

There is no single good number. It depends on the course you want. The middle ATAR is near 70. Above 80 puts you in the top fifth. Above 90 puts you in the top tenth. A good ATAR is one that gets you in.

Can I get into uni with a low ATAR?

Yes. Many courses take ATARs in the 60s and 70s. Bonus points can lift you higher. There are also paths that skip the ATAR. These include TAFE, diplomas, bridging courses and portfolios. A low ATAR is one door, not the only one.

What is the university aggregate for NTCET?

It is your score out of 90. SATAC adds the scaled scores from your best 90 credits. That is about four and a half subjects. The aggregate is ranked to give your ATAR.

How many subjects do I need for an NT ATAR?

You need at least 90 credits at Stage 2. That is usually four 20-credit subjects plus part of a fifth. At least 60 credits must come from full 20-credit subjects.

Does the Research Project count towards my ATAR?

Yes. Research Project B is worth 10 credits. It is compulsory. It can count in the flexible part of your aggregate.

How do bonus points and adjustment factors work?

Bonus points do not change your ATAR. They change your selection rank for a course. You might earn them for your area, your school, or hardship. They can lift your effective rank by 5 to 10 points. Each university sets its own scheme.

What is the highest ATAR? Why not 100?

The top ATAR is 99.95, not 100. The ATAR is a rank in steps of 0.05. To score 100 you would need to beat everyone, including yourself. So 99.95 is the top. It means you are in the top 0.05%.

Do regional or remote NT students get extra help?

Yes. Some schemes give bonus points to remote and regional students. These lift your rank for a course, not your ATAR. CDU also has direct entry.

What is scaling and why does it happen?

Scaling makes subjects fair to compare. Each subject has a different group of students. SATAC looks at how that group does across all their subjects. A strong group scales the subject up. A weaker group scales it down. It is about the group, not how hard the subject feels.

Why do different ATAR calculators give different numbers?

Each calculator models scaling in its own way. The exact scaling data is not fully public. So small gaps between tools are normal. Treat every estimate as a guide. Your real ATAR comes only on results day.

What's the difference between my ATAR and my subject marks?

Your marks are scores in each subject. Your ATAR is one rank for all of them together. Marks can sit out of 100. The ATAR runs from 0 to 99.95, but ATARs below 30.00 are reported as '30.00 or less'. One is what you got. The other is where you placed.

Should I enter my predicted or real marks?

Either works in this tool. Before results day, use your best honest guess. Base it on your recent tasks and trials. Do not enter your dream score. The estimate is only as good as the marks you put in.

Does dropping or failing a subject hurt my ATAR?

Only your best subjects count, so dropping one is often fine. You just need enough subjects left. A failed subject can count as a low or zero score. It hurts most when it is one of your counted subjects.

Is my ATAR the same across Australia?

Yes. The ATAR is national. An 85 in one state equals an 85 in another. You can use it to apply anywhere in Australia. You can also use it years later if you take a break.

Can my ATAR change after I receive it?

No. Your ATAR is set once your results are final. It will not go up or down later. You can ask for a check of your marks. The rank itself stays fixed for that year.

When do NT ATAR results come out?

SATAC releases NT ATARs in mid to late December. You get yours after your NTCET results. SATAC shares the exact date in advance.

Where do I get my real NT ATAR?

Your real ATAR comes from SATAC. You view it online in your SATAC account. Your school and the SATAC website show you how to log in.

Why is my friend’s ATAR higher with similar marks?

Subject mix and scaling explain most of the gap. Your friend may take subjects that scaled higher. Or they placed higher within their groups. Near the top, a small mark gap can mean a bigger rank gap. Focus on your own results.

Does this calculator save my marks?

No. All the maths runs in your browser. Nothing is sent to a server. Close the tab and your marks are gone. We cannot see them, store them, or share them.
