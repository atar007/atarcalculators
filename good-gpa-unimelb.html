What is a good GPA at Melbourne?

A good GPA at Melbourne depends on what you want to do with it. This guide gives realistic benchmarks on the 7-point scale, for honours, postgraduate study, scholarships and graduate jobs.

At Melbourne, a GPA of 5 or above (a credit-to-distinction average) is solid, and 6 or above (a distinction-level average) is competitive for honours, postgraduate study and graduate jobs. A 7 is exceptional. Because Melbourne uses H-grades and leads with WAM, treat a GPA as a derived figure, and check the requirement for your specific goal.

Key takeaways

  • At Melbourne, a GPA of 5+ is solid (credit-to-distinction average).
  • A GPA of 6+ is competitive for honours, postgrad and graduate jobs.
  • A GPA of 7 is exceptional.
  • A distinction average is a common strong benchmark.
  • A good GPA is the one that meets your specific goal.
  • What is competitive varies by faculty at Melbourne.

It depends on your goal

There is no single “good” GPA at Melbourne, because it depends on what you want to do. A GPA that is competitive for a graduate job might be below the threshold for a selective honours program, and vice versa.

So the useful question is not “is my GPA good?” but “is my GPA good enough for my goal?” That reframes it around the specific program, course, scholarship or job you are aiming for.

What each GPA band means

Melbourne uses H-grades rather than the usual bands. H1 is the top, H2A is 75 to 79 and H2B is 70 to 74 — together occupying the space other universities call Distinction.

Is a GPA of 5 good?

A GPA of 5 at Melbourne corresponds to H2B territory. Note that Melbourne’s Pass sits at 3, which shifts the whole distribution downward relative to other scales.

Is a GPA of 6 good?

A GPA of 6 at Melbourne is H2A territory — the level honours and postgraduate entry usually look for.

Competitive for honours

Honours entry at Melbourne typically wants a credit-to-distinction average. H1, or First Class, is a WAM of 80 or above. See our guide to the Melbourne honours GPA.

What employers look for

Employers reading a Melbourne transcript meet H-grades rather than HD and D. Expect to explain the scale on applications. Whether it still matters later is covered in does GPA matter after graduation.

A good GPA varies by faculty

Faculty rules vary at Melbourne, and the Melbourne Model means your breadth subjects may be treated differently. Check your course rules.

Your trend matters too

A rising Melbourne GPA reads better than a flat one. Melbourne leads with the WAM, so track both.

How you compare to your cohort

Your Melbourne GPA is not a rank. It is an absolute average, unlike the ATAR that got you here.

Is a GPA of 4 good?

A GPA of 4 at Melbourne is H3 territory. Because Melbourne’s Pass is 3 rather than 4, a bare pass average sits lower here than the usual scale suggests.

Your results over your whole degree

A rising Melbourne GPA reads better than a flat one. Melbourne leads with the WAM, so track both.

Setting a realistic target

A realistic Melbourne GPA target has to account for Pass counting 3. Scraping through subjects costs more at Melbourne than at universities where Pass is 4.

The honest bottom line

Honestly: Melbourne leads with the WAM, and the GPA is often a translation for forms that demand one. Do not over-optimise a number your university barely uses.

Common questions

What is a good GPA at Melbourne?

At Melbourne, a GPA of 5 or above (a credit-to-distinction average) is solid, and 6 or above (a distinction average) is competitive for honours, postgraduate study and graduate jobs. A 7 is exceptional.

What GPA is competitive for honours at Melbourne?

For honours at Melbourne, aim for at least a solid credit average, and a distinction average for competitive programs. Many honours places look for a credit-to-distinction average.

Is a credit average good at Melbourne?

Yes. A credit average, a GPA around 5, is a solid result at Melbourne that meets the entry requirement for many programs and graduate roles. For selective goals, a distinction average is more competitive.

What GPA do Melbourne employers look for?

Many graduate programs set a credit or distinction cut-off. Meeting it gets you past the initial screen. After your first role, experience matters more than GPA.
