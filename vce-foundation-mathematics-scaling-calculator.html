Quick answers

How is VCE Foundation Mathematics scaled?

VTAC scales your raw Foundation Mathematics study score against how the whole cohort performs across all their subjects. Its scaled mean is about 21.6 in 2025, versus 30 for an average subject. So it down.

Does Foundation Mathematics scale up or down?

In 2025, Foundation Mathematics down. At a study score of 30, it scales to about 20, a change of -10. What matters is how a subject compares with others, not the size of the change.

What is a good Foundation Mathematics study score?

Study scores have a mean of about 30 and a maximum of 50. A score of 30 is around the middle. A score of 40 or above is strong, and in Foundation Mathematics it scales to about 32.

Is Foundation Mathematics worth doing for the ATAR?

Only if you can score well in it. Scaling rewards a high study score, so a subject you can do well in beats a tougher one you cannot.

How Foundation Mathematics scaling works

Scaling is not a fixed bonus or penalty attached to a subject. VTAC looks at how the Foundation Mathematics cohort performs across all their subjects. It then adjusts scores so a position in Foundation Mathematics means the same as that position in any other subject.

Your ranking within Foundation Mathematics never changes. Scaling only changes how your study score contributes to your ATAR aggregate. A subject scales up when its cohort is strong across the board, and down when the cohort is broader.

Common mistakes with Foundation Mathematics scaling

The first mistake is chasing scaling instead of results. Scaling only helps if you score well. A strong study score in Foundation Mathematics beats a weak one in a higher-scaling subject.

The second is thinking one subject decides your ATAR. Scaling changes each score by a few points, then your best scores are combined. Strong results across all your subjects matter far more than the scaling of any single one.
