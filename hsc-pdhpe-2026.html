HSC PDHPE: course structure and assessment

Planning for HSC PDHPE? This guide sets out the course structure, what it covers, and how it is assessed, so you know what to expect. Always confirm the current details against NESA, since syllabus and assessment details can change.

Course overview

HSC PDHPE is a health and movement course studied over Year 12, building on the Year 11 (Preliminary) course. It develops your understanding of health and human movement through core studies of health priorities and performance, plus options.

So the Year 12 course is where your HSC mark is earned, drawing on the foundation from Year 11. The structure below is a guide; always confirm the current course with NESA, since details can be updated.

What the course covers

The Year 12 course covers the core topics Health Priorities in Australia and Factors Affecting Performance, plus two options chosen from areas such as sports medicine, improving performance, or the health of young people. Use the current NESA syllabus for the exact options.

Each part of the course has its own focus and its own dot points in the syllabus, which define exactly what you can be examined on. So use the current NESA syllabus as your definitive guide to the content.

How it is assessed

Your HSC PDHPE mark averages moderated school assessment with your aligned exam mark. Both are marked on syllabus terminology.

The exam structure

The PDHPE exam rewards syllabus language applied to a scenario. Sensible health commentary that avoids the terms cannot be credited.

Internal assessment

PDHPE internal assessment includes practical and research tasks across the year, marked on the same terminological precision.

Confirm details with NESA

Syllabus content, module names and assessment details can change between years, so this guide should be treated as an overview, not the final word. The authoritative source is always the current NESA syllabus and assessment materials.

So before you rely on any specific detail, check it against NESA’s current documents for PDHPE. This ensures you are studying the right content and preparing for the correct exam format.

Preparing for HSC PDHPE

Preparing for PDHPE means learning the syllabus terminology exactly and writing timed extended responses that use it.

Scaling and your ATAR

UAC scales PDHPE lower, because it is one of the largest and broadest cohorts in the HSC. It contributes 2 units to your best 10.

See how it scales

To see how a PDHPE mark scales, use our HSC PDHPE scaling calculator. It gives an indication of how your mark converts, so you can see how the subject fits into your ATAR.

Treat the result as indicative, since scaling changes each year, and confirm all course details with NESA.

The Year 11 foundation

The Year 12 PDHPE course builds on the Year 11 (Preliminary) course, which introduces the foundational concepts and skills. A solid grasp of the Year 11 material makes the Year 12 content far more manageable.

So do not treat Year 11 as separate or unimportant. The understanding you build there underpins your Year 12 performance, and gaps from Year 11 can make the HSC course harder than it needs to be.

Skills the course develops

PDHPE builds application of a syllabus framework to health and movement scenarios. Personal experience is not what is marked.

How your marks are weighted

PDHPE assessment weighting spreads across practical and written tasks through the year.

Planning your year

Plan your PDHPE year around the terminology. The content feels intuitive, which is exactly the trap.

From course to ATAR

PDHPE feeds health, exercise-science and education pathways.

Balancing content and skills

Success in HSC PDHPE comes from balancing knowing the material with the skill to apply it in clear responses under time pressure. Knowing the content is necessary but not sufficient; the exam also tests how well you apply and write. Both need deliberate practice.

So divide your preparation between building understanding and rehearsing responses through past papers. In PDHPE, students who build both together tend to outperform those who only revise content, since the exam rewards clear, well-supported answers.

Common questions

What is in the HSC PDHPE course?

Each part has its own syllabus dot points defining what can be examined. Always confirm the current content with NESA, since syllabus details can change.

What modules does HSC PDHPE cover?

HSC PDHPE covers the core topics Health Priorities in Australia and Factors Affecting Performance, plus two options. Confirm the current core and options with NESA, since syllabus details can change.

How is HSC PDHPE assessed?

Your HSC mark in PDHPE combines your internal school assessment mark, moderated against your cohort, with your external exam mark. These are averaged to give your HSC mark, which is aligned to a band.

What is the HSC PDHPE exam structure?

The HSC PDHPE exam typically includes multiple-choice, short-answer and extended-response sections, testing your knowledge and its application to health and performance. The exact format and mark allocation are set by NESA, so confirm them from the current syllabus and past papers.
