Quick answers

How is HSC Music 1 scaled?

UAC scales your raw Music 1 mark against how the whole Music 1 cohort performs across all of their subjects. A strong cohort lifts a subject. A broader one lowers it. Music 1's average scaled mark is about 20.8/50 per unit, versus roughly 25 across all subjects. So it down.

Does Music 1 scale up or down?

Relative to other subjects, Music 1 down. Its cohort's average scaled mark is about 20.8/50 per unit, against about 25 across the whole state. Remember that scaled marks are almost always lower than HSC marks. What matters is how a subject compares with others, not the size of the drop.

What is a good HSC Music 1 mark?

The median student scores about 84, which scales to roughly 20.4/50. Beat that and you are in the top half of the cohort. A mark in the top 10% sits near 93.

Is Music 1 worth doing for the ATAR?

Choose the subject you can score well in. A high mark in Music 1 beats a low mark in a higher-scaling subject. Scaling rewards your position in the cohort, so strong performance matters more than the subject's average.

It is the same source behind our NSW ATAR calculator. So your Music 1 scaled mark here lines up with your full ATAR estimate there.

In 2025, Music 1 down relative to other subjects. Its average scaled mark was about 20.8/50 per unit, versus roughly 25 across the whole state, below the state average. Scaled marks are lower than HSC marks in almost every subject, because HSC marks are reported high. What matters is how a subject's scaled marks compare with others, not the size of the drop.

How Music 1 scaling works

Scaling is not a fixed bonus or penalty attached to a subject. UAC looks at how the Music 1 cohort performs across all of their subjects. It then adjusts marks so a position in Music 1 means the same as that position in any other subject.

What matters is not how the scaled mark compares to your raw mark. It is how it compares to the roughly 25 per unit that an average subject sits at. In Music 1, the average scaled mark is about 20.8 per unit, versus roughly 25 across all subjects. So Music 1 down.

Common mistakes with Music 1 scaling

The first mistake is reading the drop from your HSC mark to your scaled mark as a penalty. It is not. Almost every subject scales below its HSC marks. HSC marks are reported high, and scaled marks sit around an average of 25 per unit.

The second is choosing a subject for its scaling alone. The best subject is the one you will score highly in. A strong mark in Music 1 beats a weak one in a higher-scaling subject, because scaling rewards your position in the cohort.
