The best VCE Chemistry resources

Good resources make a real difference in VCE Chemistry. This guide covers the most useful materials, from past exams to notes, and how to use them to lift your study score.

By the ATARCalculators Editorial Team ·Reviewed 4 July 2026 ·Updated July 2026 ·4 min read

The most useful VCE Chemistry resources are VCAA’s past exams and examiner reports, which are free and exam-accurate, the current study design, and quality summary notes. Chemistry rewards practising full problems, so work through past exams under timed conditions, read the examiner reports, and target your weak areas. Your teachers are a valuable resource too, since they mark to the same criteria. Use good resources systematically rather than relying on any single one.

Key takeaways

  • VCAA past exams and reports are the best resource.
  • They are free and exam-accurate.
  • Use the study design as a checklist.
  • Practise full problems under timed conditions.
  • Read the examiner reports closely.
  • Ask teachers for feedback on your work.

VCAA past exams and examiner reports

VCAA past exams and examiner reports are the essential Chemistry resource. The reports are especially useful here because Chemistry marking is precise about units, significant figures and the wording of explanations — details a textbook will not warn you about.

They are free and written by the people who set your exam.

The current study design

The Chemistry study design defines every reaction type and concept you can be assessed on. Use it as a checklist, since questions are built directly from its key knowledge.

It also protects you from the classic Chemistry time-sink: revising mechanisms in more depth than the design ever asks for.

Summary notes

Chemistry notes matter more than in Physics, because explanations must be phrased precisely. Build them around accepted wording.

How to use past exams well

Past exams reveal which Chemistry explanations are accepted and which near-synonyms are not. No textbook teaches that.

Practising Chemistry the right way

Practice in Chemistry splits two ways: stoichiometry drilled error-free, and explanations written in full. Most students skip the second.

Free vs paid resources

VCAA publishes past exams and examiner reports free. The reports are where accepted phrasing is written down.

Your teachers

Your Chemistry teacher can tell you which explanations would lose marks for imprecision — more useful than confirming the chemistry.

A study plan

Plan Chemistry revision around calculation drills, written explanations and the practical load.

See how your score scales

As you track your progress, our VCE Chemistry scaling calculator shows roughly how your score scales, so you can see how the subject fits your ATAR.

Treat the result as indicative, since scaling changes each year. Your study score is what your scaled score depends on.

How many resources do you need?

How much Chemistry practice is enough? Until stoichiometry is error-free under time and explanations use the report language automatically.

Judging resource quality

Quality in Chemistry resources means accepted wording with units and states. Loose paraphrase teaches the habit that costs marks.

Active vs passive study

Active Chemistry revision means writing explanations in full and comparing them to the reports, word by word.

When to use each resource

Schedule Chemistry around the practical load alongside calculation practice.

Making your own notes

Your own Chemistry notes are worth making if they capture exact accepted phrasing rather than your own paraphrase.

Studying with others

Group work in Chemistry helps most for checking each other's explanations against the reports.

Exam-focused resources

VCAA examiner reports are the key Chemistry resource. They are exacting about units, states and significant figures.

Common questions

What are the best resources for VCE Chemistry?

The most useful VCE Chemistry resources are VCAA past exams and examiner reports, which are free and exam-accurate, the current study design, and quality summary notes. Working through past exams under timed conditions and reading the examiner reports is the fastest way to lift your score.

Where can I get VCE Chemistry past exams?

VCAA publishes past VCE Chemistry exams and examiner reports for free. These are the real papers and the markers’ own feedback, so they are the most accurate practice available. Use them under timed conditions and mark against the reports.

Are there free notes for VCE Chemistry?

Yes. Alongside VCAA’s free past exams, reports and study design, many free summary notes exist. Making your own notes from the study design is often more valuable, and any notes you use should be checked against the current design.

How do I revise for VCE Chemistry?

Work through the study design as a checklist, practise VCAA past exams under timed conditions, mark against the examiner reports, and target your weak areas. Ask your teachers for feedback, and revise steadily rather than cramming.
