What is a good VCE Legal Studies study score?

Many VCE Legal Studies students want to know what counts as a good study score. This guide explains the benchmarks, what the average is, and how scaling changes what a score is worth.

By the ATARCalculators Editorial Team ·Reviewed 4 July 2026 ·Updated July 2026 ·4 min read

In VCE Legal Studies, the average study score is 30, a study score of 35 or above is good, and 40 or above is excellent. Because Legal Studies scales down slightly, your raw score contributes a little below face value. But what counts toward your ATAR is the scaled study score, not the raw number alone. A high ATAR usually means strong scores across several subjects, not one.

Key takeaways

  • The average study score is 30.
  • 35+ is good, 40+ is excellent.
  • A 40 is roughly the top 9 per cent.
  • Legal Studies scales down slightly.
  • The scaled score is what counts.
  • A high ATAR needs strong scores across subjects.

The average study score

By design, the average VCE study score in every subject is set to 30 each year. So a 30 is exactly average, in Legal Studies and in every other subject.

About two thirds of students score between roughly 23 and 37. This holds across subjects, because the average is fixed at 30. So a score above 30 is above average.

Is a 35 study score good in Legal Studies?

Yes. A 35 is a good Legal Studies study score, well above the average of 30 and in roughly the top quarter of students. It is a strong base for a solid ATAR, and 40 plus marks an excellent result.

What is the average study score in Legal Studies?

What Legal Studies study score do I need for a high ATAR?

There is no single score that guarantees a high ATAR, since your ATAR comes from your scaled scores across your best subjects combined. As a rough guide, scores in the 40s across your subjects point toward a high ATAR, though scaling and your subject mix matter.
