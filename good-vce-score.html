What's a good VCE score?

Every VCE student wants to know what counts as a good result. The honest answer depends on the subject and your goal. Here is what the numbers actually mean.

By the ATARCalculators Editorial Team ·Reviewed 4 July 2026 ·Updated July 2026 ·4 min read

For the VCE, a good result is one that ranks you well within your subject, and strong results in well-scaling subjects matter most. Because scaling differs by subject, the same raw result can contribute very differently to your ATAR. For a 90+ ATAR, you generally need strong, well-scaling results across your best subjects. A study score of 30 is around the average, 40 or above is roughly the top 9%, and 45 or above is around the top 2%.

Key takeaways

  • Strong results that rank you well within a subject matter most.
  • Strong results in well-scaling subjects count for more.
  • The same raw result can contribute very differently to your ATAR.
  • For a 90+ ATAR, you need strong results across your best subjects.
  • A high result in a low-scaling subject still counts — ranking well matters.
  • Your English study score, best three others, plus 10% of two more form your aggregate.

How VCE subject results work

Each VCE study reports a study score out of 50. It is already a ranking: 30 sits around the middle of the state cohort for that study, and the scale is built so scores mean the same thing across studies before scaling even starts.

What counts as a good VCE result?

A good VCE study score is one that places you high inside your own study. Because the score is already a rank, 40 means something concrete — you are near the top of the state in that study.

Victoria then counts your best four in full, so "good" has to mean good four times over.

Why scaling changes the answer

Scaling is why two 38s can be worth different amounts. VTAC adjusts each study by its cohort strength, so a 38 in Specialist converts up while a 38 in a broad-entry study converts down.

What VCE result do you need for a 90+ ATAR?

A 90+ ATAR means finishing in the top 10% of your state age group. In Victoria that generally means study scores in the high 30s and low 40s across your Primary Four, with reasonable scaling.

The 10% increments from your fifth and sixth studies help at the margin, and only at the margin.

What is the average VCE result?

Study scores are built around a mean near 30, so a score of 30 is by construction an ordinary result rather than a poor one. Most of the state sits close to it.

Is a high result in a low-scaling subject still good?

Yes. A 40 in Further Mathematics beats a 30 in Specialist, comfortably. Scaling moves a whole study; your rank inside it is yours.

It is about your best subjects

Your aggregate comes from your best four scaled study scores in full, plus 10% of your fifth and sixth, totalling around 210. A good set of scores is a strong Primary Four.

Match your results to your goal

Work backwards from your course. Find its ATAR, then ask which four study scores would produce it. The question becomes answerable immediately.

A good VCE result is personal

A good score is the one that opens your course. If it asks for 80 and your Primary Four clears it, those are good scores — whatever anyone else got.

Estimate your ATAR

Scores make sense once you see the ATAR they produce. Our VCE ATAR calculator scales each study score, builds the Primary Four, and adds the increments.

Good results by subject type

What counts as strong varies by study. A 35 in Specialist Mathematics converts better than a 35 in a broad-entry study, because of who you were ranked against.

A good result versus a high ATAR

A study score and an ATAR answer different questions. The study score ranks you inside one study, out of 50. The ATAR ranks you against your whole age group, after four scaled scores and two increments are summed.

Common questions

What is a good VCE score?

A good VCE result is one that ranks you well within your subject, and strong results in well-scaling subjects matter most. Because scaling differs by subject, the same raw result can contribute differently to your ATAR.

What VCE result do I need for a 90+ ATAR?

A 90+ ATAR usually needs strong results across your best subjects, especially well-scaling ones. There is no single guaranteed result, because your ATAR combines your best results and depends on how everyone else did.

What is the average VCE score?

In the VCE, a study score of 30 is around the average for each study. A study score of 40 or above is roughly the top 9% of that subject, and 45 or above is around the top 2%.

Is a high mark in a low-scaling subject still good?

Yes. Scaling lowers a whole subject, but your rank within it still counts. A high result means you ranked near the top, which still produces a solid scaled result towards your ATAR.
