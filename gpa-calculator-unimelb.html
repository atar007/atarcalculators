How the Melbourne WAM is calculated

Melbourne converts each mark into grade points before averaging. The complication is the vocabulary: Melbourne grades are H-grades, not the HD, D and C used elsewhere.

The formula

WAM = Σ(mark × credit points) ÷ Σ(credit points) Every unit’s actual mark (out of 100) is weighted by its credit points A GPA can also be estimated by converting marks to grade points first the University of Melbourne uses the WAM (Weighted Average Mark) as its main measure. It keeps your exact marks, weighted by how much each unit is worth. Some applications also ask for a GPA, which groups marks into grade points instead.

Step by step

  1. Enter each unit’s mark and credit points. For every unit, put in your mark out of 100 and how many credit points the unit is worth. Units are usually 6 or 12 credit points.
  2. Weight each mark by its credit points. Multiply the mark by the subject’s points. A double-weighted subject moves your WAM twice as far as a standard one.
  3. Add them up and divide. Total the weighted marks and divide by total credit points. That is your WAM out of 100.
  4. Read your result. You get your WAM, plus an indicative GPA — useful because Melbourne’s H1 scale does not translate directly onto forms expecting HD/D/C/P.

How Melbourne grades work

The University of Melbourne uses its own grade names. H1 is First Class Honours, 80 or above. H2A is 75 to 79. H2B is 70 to 74. H3 is 65 to 69. A Pass is 50 to 64. Most subjects are worth 12.5 credit points, and a standard year is 100 points.

Melbourne works in WAM, the average of your marks weighted by credit points. Like Sydney, it does not issue an official GPA, so this tool gives your WAM plus an indicative GPA for forms that need one.

For a plain WAM, use the WAM calculator. For a 7-point GPA, use the GPA calculator. The official grades and scales are on the University of Melbourne website.

Common mistakes with WAM calculations

Melbourne is the odd one out on naming and on the value of a Pass. Nearly every error below comes from translating H-grades into a scale that does not fit them.

  • Translating H-grades into HD, D and C. H1, H2A, H2B and H3 do not map cleanly onto the usual labels, and Melbourne’s Pass is worth 3 rather than 4. Translating by feel produces the wrong points.
  • Averaging your marks without weighting. A GPA weights each subject by its credit points. A 25-point subject counts twice as much as a 12.5-point one.
  • Mixing up WAM and GPA. Melbourne leads with the WAM — it is what honours classes use. The GPA is mostly a translation for forms that demand one, so know which is being asked for.
  • Forgetting which subjects count. The Melbourne Model means breadth subjects sit alongside your core. Check your course rules on whether they are included.
  • Treating an estimate as official. This tool follows the standard method. Melbourne’s own figure is the one that counts, and faculty rules vary more here than the single scale suggests.
FAQ

Common questions

Does the University of Melbourne use GPA or WAM?

Melbourne uses the WAM (Weighted Average Mark) as its primary measure, reported alongside H1/H2A/H2B/H3 grades. It can produce a GPA for external use, but the WAM is the official record. This calculator shows your WAM and an indicative GPA.

What do H1, H2A, H2B and H3 mean at Melbourne?

They are Melbourne's grade names. H1 is First Class (80+), H2A is Second Class Division A (75–79), and H2B is Second Class Division B (70–74). H3 is Third Class (65–69), P is a Pass (50–64), and N is a fail. H1 is equivalent to a High Distinction elsewhere.

How many credit points is a Melbourne subject?

A standard Melbourne subject is 12.5 credit points. So a full-time semester of four subjects totals 50 points and a full year is 100 points. Enter 12.5 for a normal subject unless your transcript shows otherwise.

What WAM do I need for H1 (First Class Honours) at Melbourne?

H1 corresponds to a WAM of 80 or above. H2A is roughly 75–79 and H2B around 70–74. Honours and many postgraduate programs reference these WAM bands, so check the specific entry requirement for your course.

What's a good WAM at Melbourne?

A WAM of 75+ (H2A or above) is strong and competitive for Honours and graduate entry; 80+ (H1) is excellent and opens scholarships and the most selective programs. 65–74 (H3) is a solid result that clears most graduate screening.

Is the GPA on this page my official Melbourne GPA?

No. It's an indicative conversion from the Melbourne bands to a 7-point scale, for situations where an overseas institution asks for a GPA. Melbourne's official measure is the WAM — for formal applications, provide your WAM or use a credential evaluator.
