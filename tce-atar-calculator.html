How your TCE scores become an ATAR

Tasmania keeps the two jobs in one place. TASC both awards your subjects and scales them, then the scaled scores are ranked to produce an ATAR. Your TCE certificate and your ATAR remain separate results: finishing the certificate does not by itself produce a rank.

Stage 1 — where a subject score comes from

Each TCE Level 3 or Level 4 subject gives you a score built from your work across the subject. Level 3 and Level 4 subjects are the ones that carry ATAR weight, which is the first thing worth checking when you count what you are taking.

Stage 2 — what TASC does with it

Scaled score = your subject score, adjusted for the strength of that subject's cohort TES = your best 5 scaled scores, added together (out of 75) ATAR = your TES converted to a rank (0 to 99.95) TASC counts your best five scaled subject scores in full, building a Tertiary Entrance Score out of a maximum of 75, with no increments.

Why the TES is out of 75, not 500

Tasmania's scale is smaller than most states, and that surprises students who compare notes across borders. Five scaled scores build to a maximum of 75, so each individual score carries proportionally more of your total than a single unit does in a 500-point system. There is no tapering and no partial contribution: the five count in full, and anything beyond the fifth is set aside.

Worked example

  1. Collect scores. Six Level 3 or 4 subjects, each producing a subject score.
  2. Scale each one. TASC adjusts every score for the strength of that subject's cohort, so two identical scores in different subjects rarely stay identical.
  3. Keep the best five. The five highest scaled scores are retained and the sixth drops out.
  4. Add to a TES. Those five scaled scores sum to a Tertiary Entrance Score out of 75.
  5. Convert to a rank. Your TES is placed against the state cohort to give the ATAR.

What this calculator does with your TCE scores

You enter a score for each subject. The calculator scales each one, keeps your best five scaled scores, sums them into a TES out of 75, and converts that total to a rank. That is TASC's sequence, run on the numbers you supply.

Because Tasmania counts five scores in full with no increments, the arithmetic after scaling is straightforward. The scaling step is the part you cannot reproduce by hand, and it is where the estimate earns its keep.

What it cannot do

Scaling is recalculated each year against the cohort that actually sat each subject, so this year's scaling does not exist yet. The estimate works from the most recent published data. Read it with a point or two of margin, as a planning number rather than a prediction.

What throws a TCE estimate off

  • Counting subjects that carry no ATAR weight. Level 3 and Level 4 subjects are the ones that build your TES. Including anything else produces a number that will not arrive.
  • Expecting a sixth subject to add to the total. Only your best five scaled scores count, and they count in full. A sixth is insurance that can replace a weaker score, not an addition.
  • Comparing a TES to another state's aggregate. Tasmania's TES tops out at 75. A TES of 60 is not "low" because other states quote totals in the hundreds — the scales are simply different sizes.
  • Confusing the TCE with the ATAR. The TCE is the certificate showing you met Tasmania's senior schooling requirements. The ATAR is a separate rank for tertiary entry.
  • Reading the last decimal seriously. The scaling that will apply to your cohort has not been calculated yet.

TCE scores and the ATAR — common questions

Is the TCE the same as the ATAR?

No. The TCE is the certificate confirming you met Tasmania's senior schooling requirements. The ATAR is a separate rank used for tertiary entry, built from your scaled subject scores.

How many subjects count towards a Tasmanian ATAR?

Five. TASC adds your best five scaled scores in full to build your Tertiary Entrance Score. Scores beyond the fifth are set aside.

What is a TES?

Your Tertiary Entrance Score is the sum of your best five scaled subject scores, out of a maximum of 75. It is the total that gets converted into your ATAR rank.

Why is the TES out of 75 when other states use hundreds?

Tasmania simply uses a smaller scale. Five scaled scores build to 75. It does not make a Tasmanian ATAR worth less; the TES is converted to the same 0 to 99.95 rank used nationally.

Which subjects carry ATAR weight in Tasmania?

Level 3 and Level 4 subjects are the ones that produce scores feeding your TES. It is worth checking the level of each subject you are taking rather than assuming.

Does a sixth subject raise my ATAR?

It cannot add to your TES, since only five scores count. It can act as insurance: if it scales better than one of your top five, it takes that place.

What is the difference between my subject score and my scaled score?

Your subject score is what TASC awards for the subject. Your scaled score is that result adjusted for the academic strength of the cohort who took it. Only scaled scores build the TES.

Why did my score scale differently from my friend's?

Because scaling reflects the strength of each subject's cohort, not how hard a subject feels. The same score in two subjects can produce two different scaled scores.
