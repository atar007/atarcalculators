Quick answers

How is VCE Accounting scaled?

VTAC scales your Accounting study score. It compares how the Accounting cohort performs across all their other subjects. A stronger cohort scales up; a broader one scales down. In 2025, a study score of 30 in Accounting became a scaled study score of about 31, so it scales up.

Does Accounting scale up or down?

Relative to other subjects, Accounting scales up. A middle study score of 30 scaled to about 31 (+1), and a 40 scaled to about 41. Scaling reflects the cohort's strength, not how hard the subject is.

What's a good Accounting study score?

A study score is a rank out of 50 with the state mean at 30. Around 30 is the middle. A score of 40 puts you in roughly the top 9% of the Accounting cohort. A score of 45 is about the top 2%. The 2025 average scaled study score in Accounting was about 31.

What does a study score of 40 scale to in Accounting?

In 2025, a study score of 40 in Accounting scaled to a VTAC study score of about 41 (+1). Use the calculator above for any study score from 0 to 50.

How Accounting scaling works

Accounting is a small, procedural study with a self-selected cohort. Here is how that cohort turns your study score into a scaled one.

The formula

Scaling moves it up or down based on how the Accounting cohort performed across all their studies.

Step by step

  1. Get your study score. Each VCE Unit 3&4 study gives a study score from 0 to 50. It already ranks you against everyone who did Accounting.
  2. Apply the scaling curve. VTAC scales your study score up or down based on the strength of the Accounting cohort. This is applied automatically here.
  3. Read your scaled study score. You get a scaled study score. This is what feeds into your ATAR aggregate, not your raw study score.

Common mistakes with Accounting scaling

Accounting is small, and small studies attract a particular set of misunderstandings.

  • Assuming a small study scales up. Size decides nothing. Accounting scales around the middle because of who takes it, not how many.
  • Treating your study score as your final result. Your score out of 50 is scaled before it counts toward your aggregate.
  • Assuming the curve is fixed. It is recalculated each year, and small cohorts move more between years than large ones do.
  • Choosing Accounting for scaling. It scales mid-table and demands procedural precision. Take it because the method suits you.
  • Expecting the exact scaled score early. Scaling depends on this year’s cohort. Use this as a guide.

VCE Accounting scaling — common questions

I got a study score of 40 in VCE Accounting — what does it scale to?

A study score of 40 in Accounting scales to about 41. For comparison, a study score of 30 scales to about 31, and a 45 to about 46. These follow the latest VTAC scaling pattern and move slightly each year with the cohort.

Does VCE Accounting scale up or down?

Accounting scales up slightly. A study score of 30 scales to about 31 (a change of +1). It’s a modest gain rather than a big one, but it’s a gain. Your scaled score sits a little above your study score across most of the range.

Why did my Accounting study score change after scaling?

VTAC scales every subject so study scores can be compared fairly for the ATAR. The adjustment reflects how strong the Accounting cohort is relative to all students. A study score of 40 in Accounting, for example, becomes about 41 after scaling. It's the scaled score, not your raw study score, that goes into your ATAR aggregate.

What study score do I need in Accounting for a scaled score of 40?

To reach a scaled score of about 40 in Accounting, you'd need a study score of roughly 39. From there, pushing your study score higher lifts your scaled score further — up to about 50 at a study score of 50.

Is VCE Accounting worth taking for a high ATAR?

Accounting gives a small scaling gain, so it's a reasonable pick — but the deciding factor should be how well you'll do in it. A high study score in Accounting contributes far more than a low one, regardless of the modest scaling.

What's a good study score in VCE Accounting?

A study score of 30 is exactly average in every VCE subject by design. In Accounting, 30 scales to about 31. A study score of 40 (around the top 9% of a subject) is strong and scales to roughly 41; 45+ is excellent. Aim as high as you realistically can — every point of study score moves your scaled score.

Can a scaled score go above 50 in Accounting?

In Accounting, scaled scores stay at or below 50 — a study score of 50 scales to about 50. Only the very strongest-scaling subjects (such as Specialist Maths) push scaled scores above 50. Most subjects, including Accounting, top out at around 50.

How is VCE Accounting scaled for the ATAR?

VTAC takes your Accounting study score and scales it. This makes it compare fairly with every other subject. It is based on how academically strong the Accounting cohort is. The stronger the field, the more the subject scales up. Your scaled scores — your best four plus increments — form the aggregate behind your ATAR.
