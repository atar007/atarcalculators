HSC Physics: course structure and assessment

Planning for HSC Physics? This guide sets out the course structure, what it covers, and how it is assessed, so you know what to expect. Always confirm the current details against NESA, since syllabus and assessment details can change.

Course overview

HSC Physics is a science course studied over Year 12, building on the Year 11 (Preliminary) course. It develops your understanding of physical principles through modules spanning mechanics, electromagnetism, light and the atom, combining theory with quantitative problem-solving.

So the Year 12 course is where your HSC mark is earned, drawing on the foundation from Year 11. The structure below is a guide; always confirm the current course with NESA, since details can be updated.

What the course covers

The Year 12 course covers Module 5 (Advanced Mechanics), Module 6 (Electromagnetism), Module 7 (The Nature of Light) and Module 8 (From the Universe to the Atom). Each builds on the Year 11 foundation and combines concepts with calculations.

Each part of the course has its own focus and its own dot points in the syllabus, which define exactly what you can be examined on. So use the current NESA syllabus as your definitive guide to the content.

How it is assessed

Your HSC Physics mark averages your moderated school assessment with your aligned exam mark. Both halves count, and the school half includes depth-study work the exam cannot reach.

The exam structure

The Physics exam is back-loaded — the extended responses at the end carry the most marks. Over-investing in the early multiple choice is the standard way to lose them.

Internal assessment

Physics internal assessment includes practical and depth-study components. It is half your HSC mark and rewards method rather than exam-day speed.

Confirm details with NESA

Syllabus content, module names and assessment details can change between years, so this guide should be treated as an overview, not the final word. The authoritative source is always the current NESA syllabus and assessment materials.

So before you rely on any specific detail, check it against NESA’s current documents for Physics. This ensures you are studying the right content and preparing for the correct exam format.

Preparing for HSC Physics

Preparing for Physics means timed problems with full working. Physics recycles question archetypes, and recognising them fast is most of the battle.

Scaling and your ATAR

UAC scales Physics up, because its students also carry Extension maths and Chemistry and perform well across all of it. Physics contributes 2 units to your best 10.

Treat the result as indicative, since scaling changes each year, and confirm all course details with NESA.

The Year 11 foundation

The Year 12 Physics course builds on the Year 11 (Preliminary) course, which introduces the foundational concepts and skills. A solid grasp of the Year 11 material makes the Year 12 content far more manageable.

So do not treat Year 11 as separate or unimportant. The understanding you build there underpins your Year 12 performance, and gaps from Year 11 can make the HSC course harder than it needs to be.

Skills the course develops

Physics builds problem selection under time — choosing the right relationship before you start writing. That is what the exam tests.

How your marks are weighted

Physics assessment weighting spreads across depth studies and topic tests through the year. Each one is banked before you sit the exam.

Planning your year

Plan your Physics year around the depth study. It is substantial, it is due when everything else is, and students routinely under-scope it.

From course to ATAR

Physics is a prerequisite or strong recommendation for engineering and physical-science degrees. It is the course that opens those doors.

Balancing content and skills

Physics sits naturally alongside Extension maths and Chemistry — that overlap is exactly why it scales as it does. Taking it alone with humanities is harder work.

Common questions

What is in the HSC Physics course?

HSC Physics covers Module 5 (Advanced Mechanics), Module 6 (Electromagnetism), Module 7 (The Nature of Light) and Module 8 (From the Universe to the Atom), combining physical concepts with quantitative problem-solving. Each part has its own syllabus dot points defining what can be examined. Always confirm the current content with NESA, since syllabus details can change.

What modules does HSC Physics cover?

HSC Physics covers four Year 12 modules: Advanced Mechanics, Electromagnetism, The Nature of Light, and From the Universe to the Atom. Confirm the current modules with NESA, since syllabus details can change.

How is HSC Physics assessed?

Your HSC mark in Physics combines your internal school assessment mark, moderated against your cohort, with your external exam mark. These are averaged to give your HSC mark, which is aligned to a band.

What is the HSC Physics exam structure?

The HSC Physics exam typically includes multiple-choice and short-answer questions and longer extended-response questions, testing both understanding and problem-solving across the modules. The exact format and mark allocation are set by NESA, so confirm them from the current syllabus and past papers.
