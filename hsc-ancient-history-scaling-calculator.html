Quick answers

How is HSC Ancient History scaled?

UAC scales your raw Ancient History mark against how the whole Ancient History cohort performs across all of their subjects. A strong cohort lifts a subject; a broader one lowers it. Ancient History's average scaled mark is about 22.4/50 per unit versus roughly 25 across all subjects. So it scales down.

Does Ancient History scale up or down?

Relative to other subjects, Ancient History scales down. Its cohort's average scaled mark is about 22.4/50 per unit, against about 25 across the whole state. HSC marks are reported high. Most students sit in Bands 4 to 6. So scaled marks come out lower than HSC marks in almost every subject. What matters is how a subject's scaled marks compare with others. The raw drop in the number is not the point.

What's a good HSC Ancient History mark?

The median student scores about 75, which scales to roughly 22.5/50. Beat that and you're in the top half of the cohort; a mark in the top 10% sits near 89.

What raw mark do I need for a Band 6 in Ancient History?

A Band 6 is an HSC (aligned) mark of 90–100 out of 100. It's set each year when NESA aligns raw marks to standards, so the exact raw mark moves year to year. This tool estimates your scaled mark, which is what actually feeds your ATAR.

How Ancient History scaling works

Scaling is not a penalty or a bonus. It puts every HSC subject on the same footing. So a mark in Ancient History can be compared fairly with a mark in any other subject. Here is exactly how your raw Ancient History mark becomes a scaled mark.

The formula

Mark per unit = your raw Ancient History mark ÷ 2 (it is a 2-unit course) Scaled mark (per unit) = mark per unit → UAC scaling curve Your ATAR uses the scaled mark, counted across your best 10 units A raw mark of 100 does not become a scaled mark of 50. Scaling reflects how the Ancient History cohort performed across all their subjects, so the scaled mark can sit above or below the raw mark per unit.

Step by step

  1. Take your raw mark out of 100. Enter your actual or predicted Ancient History HSC mark. Predicted marks are fine before results day.
  2. Convert to a per-unit mark. Ancient History is a 2-unit course. So your mark out of 100 is treated as a mark per unit (out of 50) for scaling.
  3. Apply the scaling curve. UAC maps your per-unit mark onto the Ancient History scaling curve for the year, drawn from the official percentile distribution. This is done automatically here.
  4. Read your scaled mark. You get a scaled mark per unit (out of 50). Multiply by 2 for the subject’s full contribution to your best-10-units ATAR aggregate.

Common mistakes with Ancient History scaling

These are the misunderstandings that trip students up most often.

  • Thinking a raw 100 becomes a scaled 50. Scaling is a rank-based adjustment, not a straight percentage. Even a very high Ancient History mark scales to a value set by the whole cohort, not automatically to the top.
  • Assuming Ancient History scaling is fixed forever. The curve is recalculated each year from that year’s cohort. Last year’s numbers are a close guide, not a guarantee.
  • Confusing your scaled mark with your ATAR. The scaled mark is one subject’s contribution. Your ATAR comes from your best 10 units across all subjects, then a rank.
  • Picking Ancient History only because it "scales up". A higher-scaling subject only helps if you can score well in it. A strong mark in a slightly lower-scaling subject usually beats a weak mark in a higher-scaling one.
  • Forgetting the 2-unit doubling. Your scaled mark per unit counts twice in the aggregate. When comparing subjects, compare the full 2-unit contribution, not just the per-unit figure.

HSC Ancient History scaling — common questions

I got 90 in HSC Ancient History — what scaled mark is that?

A raw HSC mark of 90 in Ancient History scales to roughly 39 out of 50 (a per-unit scaled mark). For comparison, a raw 80 scales to about 28, and a raw 95 to about 44. These are estimates from the latest UAC scaling pattern — the exact figure shifts a little each year with the cohort.

Does HSC Ancient History scale up or down?

Relative to most subjects, Ancient History scales down. Its median scaled mark is about 22/50, below the roughly 25/50 state average, because on average the cohort is broader. That doesn’t make it a bad choice. A high mark in Ancient History still scales better than a low mark in a ‘strong’ subject.

Why is my scaled mark lower than my HSC mark in Ancient History?

Because the two numbers measure different things. Your raw HSC mark (out of 100) is aligned to performance bands. Your scaled mark (out of 50 per unit) places you against every student in the state. It reflects how strong the Ancient History cohort is. A raw 90 in Ancient History, for instance, becomes about 39/50. Scaled marks sitting below raw HSC marks in the middle of the range is normal and happens in nearly every subject.

What raw mark do I need in Ancient History for a scaled mark of 40?

A scaled mark of about 40/50 is a strong contribution to your ATAR aggregate. In Ancient History, reaching it takes a raw HSC mark of roughly 91 out of 100. Lifting your raw mark above that pushes your scaled mark higher still, up to about 49/50 at the very top.

Is HSC Ancient History worth taking for a high ATAR?

Ancient History scales down, so it won't lift your ATAR through scaling. But the best subject is still the one you’ll score highly in. A Band 6 in Ancient History contributes more than a Band 4 in a high-scaling subject. Pick it on interest and on where your marks will land, not to chase or avoid scaling.

What scaled mark is a Band 6 in Ancient History?

A Band 6 in Ancient History starts at a raw HSC mark of 90, which scales to about 39/50. The very top of the band (a raw 100) scales to roughly 49/50. So even within Band 6 there's a real scaled-mark gap between a 90 and a 100.

What HSC mark puts me in the top 10% of Ancient History?

Roughly the top 10% of Ancient History students score a raw HSC mark of about 89 or higher. Your position within the cohort is what drives your scaled mark — the stronger your rank, the higher your scaled result.

How is HSC Ancient History scaled for the ATAR?

UAC scales your Ancient History marks (exam plus moderated assessment) so they compare fairly with every other subject. The scaling reflects how academically strong the Ancient History cohort is — the stronger the field, the higher the subject scales. It's your scaled marks, not your raw HSC marks, that feed into your ATAR aggregate.
