TAS ATAR release date 2026: when do results come out?

Waiting on your TCE results? This guide covers the timing you can expect for Tasmanian ATARs in 2026, how TASC delivers them, and when University of Tasmania offers follow.

By the ATARCalculators Editorial Team ·Reviewed 3 July 2026 ·Updated July 2026 ·6 min read

Key takeaways

  • Expect TAS ATARs in mid-to-late December 2026, matching recent years.
  • TASC releases your TCE results and your ATAR.
  • You access your results through the TASC portal.
  • University of Tasmania offers usually follow soon after.
  • Always confirm the exact date on the TASC and UTAS sites.

When are 2026 TAS ATAR results released?

TAS ATAR results come out each year in mid-to-late December. Based on recent years, expect them in the second half of December 2026.

TASC sets and announces the exact date closer to the time. So treat late December as a guide, not a fixed promise. A day or two of movement year to year is normal.

The safest move is to check the official TASC and University of Tasmania pages in November or December 2026 for the confirmed date and time.

What time of day are TAS results out?

Results are usually available from the morning on release day. TASC publishes the exact time alongside the date, so check its page for the confirmed release time.

It is normal for the portal to be busy right when results go live. If the site is slow, wait a short while and try again, rather than refreshing over and over.

How will I receive my TAS ATAR?

Your TCE results and your ATAR are released by TASC through its online portal. You log in with your student account.

Keep your login handy before results day, so you are not hunting for it on the morning. If you cannot log in, TASC has support to help you recover access.

Your TCE results and ATAR

Your TCE results show how you did in each subject. Your ATAR is the rank TASC works out from your TES.

Both come from TASC, so you get them in one place. See our TAS ATAR guide for how your subject results become your ATAR.

When do University of Tasmania offers follow?

After ATARs are released, the University of Tasmania makes offers. These usually follow soon after results, with later rounds for courses that still have places.

If you do not get your first choice straight away, keep watching. Later rounds and pathway options are still worth considering. Use the time to review your preferences.

What to do while you wait

The wait is stressful, so plan for it. Have a backup course in mind, in case your first choice needs a higher ATAR than you get.

It also helps to know your options early. Read our TAS cutoffs guide so you know which courses suit a range of results. That way, whatever your ATAR, you have a plan ready.

What happens after your ATAR

Your ATAR is not the end of the process. After results, you finalise your application to the University of Tasmania, and offers follow, with main offers around the start of the new year. So there is a gap between getting your ATAR and receiving an offer.

Use that gap wisely. Once you know your ATAR, review the courses you have applied for and make sure they match your result and your goals. Because you apply to UTAS directly, it is worth confirming your choices and checking any guaranteed-entry thresholds.

Preparing for results day

Results day can be stressful, so plan for it. Know in advance how and when you will access your results, and make sure your login details are ready. Decide who you want around you when you check, whether that is family or on your own.

It also helps to know your options ahead of time. Look up the requirements for the courses you want and a few alternatives, so that whatever your ATAR, you already understand your next step. Going in prepared takes some of the pressure out of the moment.

If your results are not what you hoped

If your ATAR is lower than you hoped, remember it is one pathway among many. The University of Tasmania offers alternative entry options, enabling and foundation programs, and school-recommendation schemes that can lead to a degree. An unexpected result is a setback, not a dead end.

Take a little time, then look at your options calmly. Many students reach the course they want through a second pathway, and UTAS has staff who can talk you through the alternatives. There is almost always a route forward.

The results-to-offer timeline

It helps to hold the whole timeline in mind. Your results and ATAR arrive in December, you confirm your UTAS application around then, and offers follow near the start of the new year. So the process runs over several weeks, not a single day.

Knowing this takes some pressure off results day itself. Your ATAR is an important milestone, but it is one step in a sequence, and you still have time to confirm your choices before offers are made.
