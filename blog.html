Guides, common questions

Who writes these ATAR guides?

Every guide is written and maintained by the ATARCalculators Editorial Team, an independent group that works with Australian senior-secondary and tertiary-admissions data. We source every figure from official reports and review each guide when new data is released.

Are the guides accurate and up to date?

Yes. Each guide is built from official sources — UAC, VTAC, QTAC, SCSA, SATAC, TASC, BSSS and ACARA — carries a 'last reviewed'. Date, and is updated when new scaling or assessment reports are published.

Do these guides replace official advice?

No. They explain how the system works and help you plan. But your official ATAR, results and cut-offs come from your state's tertiary admissions body and the universities themselves. We link to those official sources throughout.

Are the guides free?

Completely. No signup, no paywall, no email gate. Every guide and every calculator on the site is free to use.

Which guide should I start with?

If you're in Year 12, start with your state's ATAR guide and the subject-scaling guides for your subjects. Parents of younger students should start with the NAPLAN and selective-school guides. Each guide links to the right calculator so you can run your own numbers.
