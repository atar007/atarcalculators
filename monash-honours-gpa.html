Monash GPA requirements for honours and postgrad

Planning honours or postgraduate study at Monash? This guide sets out the typical WAM and GPA requirements, explains what First Class Honours means at Monash, and shows how entry and honours classes differ.

Key takeaways

  • Honours entry usually needs a credit-to-distinction average (GPA 5+).
  • First Class (H1) generally needs a WAM around 80+.
  • H2A around 75, H2B around 70.
  • Honours classes are based on your WAM.

  • Requirements vary by program — always check.

Entry into honours

Entry into an honours year at Monash usually requires a credit-to-distinction average in your undergraduate degree, often around a WAM in the 60–79 range, or roughly a GPA of 5 or higher. More selective programs ask for more.

Some programs weight your marks in the relevant discipline more heavily than your overall average, so strong results in your major matter. If honours is your goal, aim for at least a solid credit average, and check the specific program’s threshold.

Honours is often the gateway to research and to a PhD, so meeting the entry requirement matters beyond the year itself. Plan for it across your degree, not just in your final year.

First Class Honours at Monash

First Class Honours at Monash generally needs a WAM around 80 or above, with H2A around 75 and H2B around 70. Honours classes are based on your WAM rather than your GPA.

The honours classes

Monash honours classes run H1, H2A, H2B and H3. Note Monash uses H-class labels for honours even though its coursework grades are HD, D, C and P.

Entry vs your honours class

Entry and class are different things at Monash. Entry needs a credit-to-distinction average; the class you graduate with depends on your honours-year WAM.

GPA for postgraduate study

Postgraduate coursework at Monash typically reads your GPA. Research degrees care about the honours class. Our guide to GPA requirements for honours and masters sets out the thresholds.

Research places and scholarships

Research pathways at Monash usually want H1 or a strong H2A. That is the gateway to a PhD, not the GPA.

Does Monash use WAM or GPA for entry?

Monash honours classes use the WAM, not the GPA. Students routinely optimise the wrong number — see Monash WAM vs GPA for the difference.

If you are just below the requirement

If you are borderline at Monash, the WAM is what to lift. Remember Monash’s Distinction band starts at 70, so the marks map differently than you may expect.

Planning ahead

Plan your Monash honours year around the WAM. It is a single year and the thesis carries substantial weight. Our guide to improving your university GPA covers the habits that move it.

Knowing both numbers, and the threshold you need, lets you plan your remaining units to reach your goal.

Why honours needs a strong average

Honours matters at Monash because it is the research pathway. Without it, a PhD generally requires a masters instead.

The research component

Research pathways at Monash usually want H1 or a strong H2A. That is the gateway to a PhD, not the GPA.

Alternative pathways to postgraduate study

Monash honours pathways vary by faculty — some are embedded, some are a separate year. Check which yours is early.

When to apply

Apply for Monash honours in the year before. Places are limited and supervisors fill up.

Common questions

What GPA or WAM do I need for honours at Monash?

Honours entry at Monash usually needs a credit-to-distinction average, often a WAM in the 60–79 range or roughly a GPA of 5 or higher. Selective programs ask for more, and some weight your marks in the relevant discipline.

What is First Class Honours at Monash?

First Class Honours (H1) at Monash generally needs a WAM around 80 or above, awarded on your honours-year performance. H2A is around 75 and above, and H2B around 70 and above.

What GPA do I need for postgraduate study at Monash?

Postgraduate coursework at Monash often asks for a pass-to-credit average as a minimum, while selective courses expect a distinction average, roughly a GPA of 6. Research degrees usually require honours or a research masters.

Does Monash admit honours on WAM or GPA?

Monash bases its honours classes on your WAM, and reports a 7.0 GPA alongside it. So for Monash’s own decisions, WAM is the key figure; a GPA is mainly for external audiences.
